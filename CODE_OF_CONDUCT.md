# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

unifly is built for everyone who manages networks. We are committed to providing a welcoming,
inclusive, and harassment-free experience for all contributors and community members, regardless
of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression,
level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race,
caste, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

Discrimination, harassment, and exclusion have no place here. Full stop.

## Our Standards

**Positive behavior includes:**

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Respecting differing viewpoints and experiences
- Giving and receiving constructive feedback gracefully
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy toward other community members

**Unacceptable behavior includes:**

- Sexualized language or imagery, and unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information (physical or electronic addresses) without explicit
  permission
- Any conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## AI and Tooling

We embrace AI as a development tool. Contributions may be authored with the help of AI assistants,
and that is explicitly welcome. What matters is the quality of the contribution, not whether a
human or an AI typed the first draft.

That said:

- **AI-assisted contributions are held to the same quality bar** as any other contribution.
  AI-generated code must be reviewed, tested, and understood by the submitter.
- **You are responsible for what you submit.** If an AI tool produces incorrect, insecure, or
  license-violating code and you submit it, that's on you.
- **Attribution is appreciated.** If an AI tool did the heavy lifting, a note in the PR
  description is a nice thing to do. It's not required.

We believe AI makes it possible for more people to contribute to open source, and that's a
good thing.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces (GitHub, Discord, mailing lists, events)
and when an individual is representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project
maintainers at **stef@hyperbliss.tech**. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly
and fairly. Maintainers are obligated to maintain confidentiality with regard to the reporter.

Project maintainers who do not follow or enforce the Code of Conduct in good faith may face
temporary or permanent consequences as determined by other members of the project's leadership.
